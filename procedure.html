Procedure

Introduction to Entropy

In this exercise, you will observe the evolution of a simple particle system from an ordered state to a more probable mixed state. The simulation represents a closed system in which particles move randomly over time.

Step 1 — Initial Observation

Examine the initial configuration of the particle system. Note the separation of particle types within the container.

Step 2 — Observation

Record qualitative observations regarding the organization of the system before the divider is removed.

Step 3 — Prediction

Before running the simulation, predict how the system will evolve once the barrier between regions is removed.

Step 4 — Run Simulation

Activate the simulation and observe the motion of particles within the container. Allow the simulation to proceed until the particle distribution appears stable.

The system is considered closed. No particles enter or leave the container during the simulation.

Step 5 — Record Observations

Record observations regarding the final state of the system.

Step 6 — Repeat Trials

Run the simulation three times and compare the resulting particle distributions.

Step 7 — Analysis

Determine whether the final particle arrangements are identical between trials. Compare the degree of organization present in the initial and final states. Consider which state contains a greater number of possible microscopic arrangements.

Step 8 — Post-Lab Asessment

Complete the post-laboratory assessment and answer all analysis questions.

Ensure all observations are recorded before proceeding.
